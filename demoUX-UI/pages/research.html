Research

Evidence-based research on plastic waste in Japanese supermarkets

Research Overview

Our research focuses on understanding the scope and impact of plastic packaging in Japanese supermarkets. Through systematic surveys and data collection across 6+ supermarket locations, we've gathered comprehensive evidence about plastic waste practices and their environmental impact.

Our methodology combines quantitative data collection (packaging counts, material analysis) with qualitative observations (store practices, customer behavior). This multi-faceted approach provides a complete picture of the plastic waste problem and helps identify practical solutions.

All research data is documented in detailed Google Docs reports, available for public access. We believe in transparency and evidence-based advocacy, which is why we make all our findings publicly available.

Key Findings

Excessive Packaging

85%

Of fresh produce items use unnecessary plastic packaging, including double-wrapping and individual plastic containers.

Non-Recyclable Materials

60%

Of plastic packaging used is not recyclable in local recycling programs, contributing to landfill waste.

Alternative Availability

15%

Only 15% of supermarkets surveyed offer plastic-free alternatives or bulk buying options for common products.

Consumer Awareness

72%

Of surveyed consumers expressed concern about plastic waste but feel they lack alternatives when shopping.

Our Methodology

1

Location Selection

We selected 6+ supermarkets representing different chains, sizes, and customer demographics in the Toyonaka area. This diversity ensures our findings are representative of typical supermarket practices in Japan.

2

Data Collection

Team members visited each location multiple times to document packaging practices. We recorded product categories, packaging types, material composition, and recyclability information. Photos were taken for visual documentation.

3

Customer Surveys

We conducted informal surveys with shoppers to understand consumer awareness, preferences, and challenges related to plastic-free shopping. This qualitative data helps contextualize our quantitative findings.

4

Analysis & Reporting

All data was compiled and analyzed to identify patterns, trends, and opportunities for improvement. Findings are documented in detailed Google Docs reports with recommendations for supermarkets, policymakers, and consumers.
