Our Journey

LATE 2024

SWAP Initiative Founded

Group of high school students came together with a shared vision to tackle plastic waste in Japanese supermarkets. Initial planning and research methodology developed focusing on manager interviews and on-site documentation.

JANUARY 20, 2025

First Supermarket Site Visit

Conducted initial research visit to Supermarket 1 with comprehensive photo documentation of plastic packaging practices. Observed both in-store wrapping and distributor packaging methods.

FEBRUARY 1, 2025

First Manager Interview Completed

Successfully completed manager interview at Supermarket 1. Learned about their reusable packaging system that returns materials to suppliers and municipal recycling. Identified key challenges including customer expectations and sanitation concerns.

ONGOING

Partnership with Toyonaka City Hall

Established partnership with Toyonaka City Hall environmental department. Collaborating on community workshops and city-wide sustainability initiatives to expand awareness and implement plastic reduction solutions.

KEY DISCOVERY

Simple Solution Identified

Through manager interviews, identified practical solution: training employees to ask customers before adding extra bags could significantly reduce plastic consumption without requiring major system changes or extended implementation periods.

PRESENT

Expanding Research & Advocacy

Continuing manager interviews and site visits across 6+ supermarket locations. Building evidence-based recommendations for corporate-level policy changes while promoting practical, immediate solutions like employee training programs.
