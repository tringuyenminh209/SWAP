About Us

A high school student-led initiative tackling plastic waste in Japanese supermarkets

Our Story

SWAP (Stop Waste And Plastic) was founded by a group of passionate high school students who recognized the urgent need to address plastic waste in Japanese supermarkets. What started as a school project quickly evolved into a full-fledged movement to create lasting change in our community.

We believe that young people have the power to drive environmental change. Through research, advocacy, and community engagement, we're working to reduce single-use plastics and promote sustainable alternatives in supermarkets across Japan.

Our approach combines grassroots activism with evidence-based research. We've visited multiple supermarket locations, documented packaging practices, collected data, and engaged with both consumers and business owners to understand the challenges and opportunities for reducing plastic waste.

Our Journey

From a school project to a community movement - here's how SWAP evolved

SEPTEMBER 2024

The Idea Sparks

SWAP was born during a high school environmental science class discussion about Japan's plastic waste challenge. Founder Annabelle Siddons recognized the need for student-led action targeting supermarket packaging.

NOVEMBER 2024

First Research Visit

Conducted initial supermarket research, documenting packaging practices and interviewing managers. The findings revealed the complexity of the plastic packaging issue and inspired our evidence-based approach.

JANUARY 2025

Official Launch & Partnerships

SWAP officially launched with a team of dedicated volunteers. Established partnerships with ECC Volunteer Center and began systematic research across 6+ supermarket locations.

MARCH 2025

Toyonaka City Hall Partnership

Secured official partnership with Toyonaka City Hall's Environmental Department, gaining governmental support and resources to expand our community impact and policy advocacy efforts.

ONGOING

Growing Movement

Today, SWAP continues to grow with 75+ active volunteers, expanding research, advocacy campaigns, and community education programs. Our movement is gaining momentum across Osaka and beyond.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SWAP

Who can join SWAP?
SWAP welcomes anyone passionate about reducing plastic waste! While we started as a high school student initiative, we now have volunteers from all backgrounds including university students, young professionals, and community members. No prior experience in environmental activism is required.
How much time commitment is required?
We offer flexible volunteer opportunities! Most volunteers commit 4-8 hours per month, but you can participate as much or as little as your schedule allows. We have one-time events, ongoing projects, and leadership roles available.
Do I need to speak Japanese?
Not necessarily! While some of our work involves engaging with Japanese-speaking supermarket managers and community members, we have many roles that don't require Japanese fluency. We have a bilingual team and can match you with tasks that fit your language abilities.
What kind of activities does SWAP do?
SWAP's work includes: supermarket packaging research and data collection, community workshops and educational events, advocacy meetings with businesses and government, social media campaigns, petition drives, and collaboration with partner organizations. There's something for everyone!
How can businesses partner with SWAP?
We welcome partnerships with businesses committed to sustainability! Supermarkets can work with us on packaging audits and pilot programs. Other businesses can sponsor events, provide resources, or collaborate on community initiatives. Contact us at swapplastictogreen@gmail.com to discuss partnership opportunities.
Has SWAP achieved any concrete results?
Yes! We've documented packaging practices at 6+ supermarket locations, reached over 8,000 people through events and campaigns, established official partnerships with Toyonaka City Hall and ECC, and contributed research to municipal sustainability planning. Some supermarkets we've engaged with have started pilot programs for plastic reduction.
How can I support SWAP if I can't volunteer?
There are many ways to support our mission! Sign our petition, follow us on Instagram (@swapplastictogreen), share our posts to spread awareness, choose products with minimal packaging when shopping, bring reusable bags and containers, and talk to your friends and family about plastic waste. Every action counts!
